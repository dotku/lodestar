## Part 4 — Procurement paths for a Series-A AI startup

- **DIU prototype OTA → production OTA.** Strongest narrative path. JSDT model (ASI + Watchtower Labs, Jan 2026) is the template: CSO assessment → prototype OTA → production OTA at scale. Fastest non-FAR entry for AI-native software. Typical prototype $1–10M; production can scale to 9 figures without re-competition.
- **Tradewinds Solutions Marketplace (CDAO → now USD(R&E)).** $3.26B+ awards facilitated. "Awardable" status reachable via video-pitch evaluation. Best fit for AI-native vendor lacking a primary acquirer. Active Apr 1, 2025 – May 31, 2026.
- **SBIR Phase I → II → III.** Phase I $50–200K, Phase II $1–2M, Phase III sole-source and uncapped (e.g., DEFCON AI's USAF Phase III; Muon Space's $44.6M USSF Phase III OTA, Oct 2025). Cleanest legal path to sustained sole-source revenue for a small business.
- **AFWERX / SOFWERX / NavalX.** Service-specific innovation arms with their own SBIR-Open and STRATFI/TACFI bridges. Best when target buyer is a single service component.
- **CDAO / USD(R&E) direct via Tradewinds + CSO.** Post Aug-2025 realignment, AI procurement is increasingly routed through USD(R&E)/CDAO joint instruments — useful for cross-service capabilities but slower contracting cycles.
- **Prime subcontracting (Lockheed, RTX, Booz, SAIC).** Lowest-friction first revenue but lowest margin, highest IP-leakage risk, weakest standalone investor narrative. Parallel channel only, never the headline.

## Part 5 — Compliance gate-readiness checklist

- **FedRAMP Moderate.** Baseline for any SaaS handling federal data. 12–18 mo, ~$500K–$1M.
- **FedRAMP High.** Prerequisite for DoD IL4/IL5 reciprocity. 18–36 mo, $1.2M–$3M+ total; $75K–$200K/yr maintenance.
- **DoD IL2.** Public/non-CUI; FedRAMP Moderate maps directly. Table stakes.
- **DoD IL4.** CUI / non-NSS. FedRAMP High PA accepted as baseline + Cloud Computing SRG controls. **US-persons-only access required.**
- **DoD IL5.** Higher-sensitivity CUI / NSS / mission-critical. FedRAMP High + FedRAMP+ C/CEs + SRG. **US-citizens-only.** Realistic ceiling for a logistics AI vendor handling sustainment data.
- **DoD IL6.** SECRET / classified. SIPRNet-resident. Out of scope for Series A.
- **CMMC 2.0 Level 1.** Self-assessment, FCI only. Enforceable since **Nov 10, 2025**.
- **CMMC 2.0 Level 2.** CUI handling; self-assessment now, **third-party C3PAO assessment required Nov 10, 2026** (Phase 2).
- **CMMC 2.0 Level 3.** Higher-tier CUI / APT-resistant; enforceable **Nov 10, 2027**. Out of scope for Series A unless pursuing nuclear / weapons-systems primes.
- **ITAR / EAR.** Required if technical data or software is export-controlled (likely for sustainment of weapons platforms). DDTC registration is cheap/fast; operational discipline is the hard part.
- **SOC 2 Type II.** Not DoD-required but commercial buyers and primes will request it. Cheap baseline ($30–80K).

**Scoping note:** As of Nov 10, 2025, CMMC clauses appear in **solicitations only at the contracting officer's discretion** during Phase 1. **Not yet universal across all DoD prime contracts.** Site copy should phrase CMMC enforcement as "phased rollout begun Nov 10, 2025; mandatory Level 2 third-party certification Nov 10, 2026" — not as already-universal.

## Summary verdict

The thesis is solid: macro anchors are real and dated — DLA's $52.6B → $55.4B obligation growth, CDAO realignment under USD(R&E), JSDT awards, Palantir's $10B Army EA, Govini's $919M SCRIPTS BPA + $1B unicorn round — together demonstrate that **DoD logistics-AI has crossed from R&D into production-scale procurement during 2025**.

**Strongest verifiable anchors for an investor pitch:**
- JSDT awards (Jan 2026)
- Govini SCRIPTS BPA ($919M, Apr 2025)
- CMMC 2.0 effective Nov 10, 2025
- Rune Technologies' $24M Series A (Jul 2025)

These form a clean "the market is being created right now" narrative.

**Biggest factual risk for the pitch page:** **conflating contract ceilings with obligated dollars.** Palantir $10B, JWCC $9B, Anduril $20B Army ceiling, Govini $919M BPA are all *not-to-exceed*, not guaranteed spend. Investor-savvy readers will downgrade credibility if presented as committed revenue.

**Secondary risk:** USTRANSCOM TWCF size figure should be re-verified against most recent USTRANSCOM Agency Financial Report before quoting any specific dollar amount.
